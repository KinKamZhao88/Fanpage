Edward Christopher Sheeran

Biography

Edward Christopher Sheeran was born in Halifax, West Yorkshire on February 17, 1991. When he was a child, he moved with his family from Hebden Bridge to Framlingham in Suffolk. Sheeran sang in a local church choir from the age of four, learned to play guitar during his time at Rishworth School, and began writing songs while at Thomas Mills High School in Framlingham. He was accepted at the National Youth Theatre in London as a teenager. He sucessfully auditioned for Youth Music Theatre UK in 2007 and joined their production of Frankenstein in Plymouth. Sheeran began recording music in 2004, and indepedently released his first collection of work, Spinning Man. On April 26, 2011, Sheeran appeared on the TV music show Later... with Jools Holland where he performed his debut single "The A Team". Six weeks later, "The A Team" was released as a digital download in the UK. "The A Team" entered the UK Singles Chart at number three, selling over 58,000 copies in the first week. It was the best selling debut single and the overall eighth-best selling single of 2011, selling 801,000 copies.
